The story behind the data: Access to internet and mobile phone networks are an important measure of development. They allow citizens to connect, look up information, and are crucial tools for economic progress. Connecting through the world-wide web or phone is no longer a luxury, but a necessity. This visualisation shows how different African nations over time have become more connected. Although progress has been made, the data shows that a lot of countries still score poorly on these variables. The two graphs combined show that we should be optimistic about efforts to increase connectivity in Africa, but also that there is a long way to go. Source: The African Development Bank socio-economic database
